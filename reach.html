Reach is a goal oriented savings platform providing insight and data that makes saving easy for everyone.

The app allow users to set a saving goal and budgets for different spending categories. The weekly difference between their spendings and total budget gets added to their savings. Users can tracks their expenses by syncing the app with their bank accounts, and access statistics based on their habits.

Ideation

Reach targets adults wishing to save money quickly for a specific reason, especially people who are used to technology, but do not like working with their finances.

Therefore, Reach had to provide expert advice to help users reach their goals while also remaining approachable & accessible to anyone.

Coming Up With The Concept

The objective for this first iteration was to give users access to information and data they could leverage to execute meaningful change while also keeping the platform relatively simple.

The first step was to make a list of essential features to be included in the app before mapping out user flows to be illustrated in the final prototype. Apart from the start-up process, user flows illustrated in the mockups and final prototype included 1. editing the saving goal from the dashboard 2. accessing financial transactions and filtering the data showed 3. accessing insight about one’s finances.

Wireframing & Testing

After brainstorming and sketching multiple low-fidelity iterations, I created my first medium-fidelity wireframes in Figma.

The medium-fidelity wireframes were then turned into a clickable prototype for usability testing. The objective was to assess the general intuitiveness and learnability of the app with new users that fit the target audience profile. To do so, I asked users to complete a short series of tasks on the prototype; they had to complete the action within 30 seconds for the task to be considered successful.
